Jacopo di Casentino

peintre italien
- Date de naissance : 1297
- Lieu de naissance : Pratovecchio
- Date de mort : 1358
- Lieu de mort : Pratovecchio

Rôle : Giorgio Vasari, a 16th-century biographer of artists, incorrectly identified him as a fellow Aretine from Pratovecchio and as a member of the Landini family. He was probably trained in Florence. One of his important works is the St. Michael altarpiece (ca. 1322) for the Velluti chapel in Santa Croce, Florence. He is particularly known for his small-scale narrative scenes, and is identified with the 'Miniaturist Tendency.' Bernardo Daddi, Ugolino di Nerio, and, in particular, Giotto were important influences. Comment on works: religious
