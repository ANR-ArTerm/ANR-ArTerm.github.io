Rôle : Italian family of artists. The family, originally from Cremona, had settled around the middle of the 15th century in Bologna, where its male members earned their living as tradesmen. The influence of the Carracci was felt at Bologna from ca. 1582 when Agostino and Annibale opened an academy of art in the studio of Ludovico. The Carracci’s artistic reform was rooted in a profound disaffection with current Mannerist practice and an equally deep commitment to north Italian naturalism and colore. In order to escape the sterility of maniera, they turned to the study of nature on the one hand and of the paintings of non-Mannerist artists such as Correggio, Titian, and Veronese on the other.
